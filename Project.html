Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad

The Narendra Modi Stadium, located in Motera, Ahmedabad, is the largest cricket stadium in the world. It has a massive seating capacity of 132,000 spectators, surpassing even the Melbourne Cricket Ground. Originally known as the Sardar Patel Stadium, it was renamed in 2021 after India's current Prime Minister. The stadium was reconstructed and inaugurated in February 2020. Built by Larsen & Toubro, its design was crafted by the global architecture firm Populous. It features 11 cricket pitches, modern drainage, LED lighting, and luxurious facilities. The stadium is owned and operated by the Gujarat Cricket Association. It regularly hosts international matches and is home to the Gujarat Titans IPL team. With its state-of-the-art infrastructure, it has become a major hub for cricket in India. The venue is easily accessible via metro and road, making it a popular destination for sports fans.

Maharashtra Cricket Association Stadium, Pune

The Maharashtra Cricket Association Stadium, located in Gahunje near Pune, is a modern, state-of-the-art cricket venue inaugurated in 2012. It serves as the home ground for the Maharashtra cricket team and also functions as the headquarters of the Maharashtra Cricket Association. Designed by renowned architect Sir Michael Hopkins and constructed by Shapoorji Pallonji & Co. Ltd., the stadium boasts a seating capacity of around 55,000 spectators. Since its opening, it has hosted several important matches, including Test matches, ODIs, T20Is, and games during the 2023 ICC World Cup, making it a significant venue in India’s cricketing landscape.

Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai

Wankhede Stadium, located in the heart of Mumbai, is one of India's most iconic cricket venues. Established in 1974, it was constructed to address disputes between the Mumbai Cricket Association and the Cricket Club of India over ticket allocations at the Brabourne Stadium. Named after S. K. Wankhede, the then-secretary of the Mumbai Cricket Association, the stadium was designed by architect Shashi Prabhu and constructed in approximately 13 months. It officially opened in time for the final Test between India and the West Indies in 1975.

The passionate and knowledgeable cricket fans of Chennai add to the stadium’s unique atmosphere, making it a revered venue in the world of cricket.
